Giraffe

Giraffes are the tallest mammals on Earth. Their legs alone are taller than many humans—about 6 feet. They can run as fast as 35 miles an hour over short distances, or cruise at 10 mph over longer distances. A giraffe's neck is too short to reach the ground

  • Scientific name: Giraffa
  • Gestation period: 13 – 15 months
  • Height: Male: 5 – 6 m (Adult), Female: 4.6 m (Adult)
  • Mass: Male: 1,200 kg, Female: 830 kg

The giraffe (Giraffa) is a genus of African even-toed ungulate mammals, the tallest living terrestrial animals and the largest ruminants. The genus currently consists of one species, Giraffa camelopardalis, the type species. Seven other species are extinct, prehistoric species known from fossils.
